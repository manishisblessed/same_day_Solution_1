## PART I — INTRODUCTION AND HIERARCHY

### Section 1 — Purpose and Scope

**1.1** This Annexure B ("**Role & Service Schedule**") defines the roles, responsibilities, service entitlements, operational obligations, and inter-partner relationships within the SAMEDAY channel partner network.

**1.2** This Schedule forms an integral part of the Master Channel Partner Agreement (SDS-MCPA / v1.0) dated _______________ between SAMEDAY SOLUTIONS PRIVATE LIMITED ("**SAMEDAY**") and the Channel Partner identified in DOC-1.

**1.3** In the event of conflict between this Schedule and the principal agreement, the order of precedence shall be: (a) regulatory mandate; (b) principal agreement; (c) this Schedule; (d) operational circulars.

### Section 2 — Partner Hierarchy

**2.1** The SAMEDAY partner network operates on a tiered structure as follows:

```
SAMEDAY SOLUTIONS PRIVATE LIMITED (Platform)
        │
        ├── Master Distributor (MD)
        │       ├── Distributor
        │       │       ├── Retailer
        │       │       └── Retailer
        │       └── Distributor
        │
        ├── Distributor (direct under SAMEDAY)
        │       └── Retailer
        │
        ├── Retailer (direct under SAMEDAY)
        │
        └── Partner (API) — parallel integration track
```

**2.2** Partner (API) operates on a technology integration model and does not participate in the physical retail hierarchy unless expressly dual-onboarded with written SAMEDAY approval.

**2.3** No Channel Partner shall onboard sub-partners outside the authorised hierarchy or assign partner codes without SAMEDAY's system-generated allocation.

## PART II — ROLE DEFINITIONS AND GENERAL OBLIGATIONS

### Section 3 — Common Obligations (All Roles)

**3.1** Every Channel Partner, regardless of role, shall:

**(a)** Comply with the Channel Partner Agreement, Annexure A (Compliance Policy), Annexure C (Fee & Settlement Schedule), and all applicable RBI, NPCI, and statutory requirements;

**(b)** Use SAMEDAY-provided credentials, devices, and APIs only for authorised purposes;

**(c)** Maintain accurate records and cooperate with audits, inspections, and compliance enquiries;

**(d)** Report suspicious activity, device loss, and security incidents within twenty-four (24) hours;

**(e)** Not sub-delegate, sublicense, or franchise SAMEDAY services without prior written consent;

**(f)** Ensure grievance redressal contact details are accessible to end customers at retail touchpoints;

**(g)** Bear responsibility for all transactions initiated through its partner code, login, device, or API credentials.

**3.2** SAMEDAY may assign a risk tier and transaction limits based on partner role, history, and compliance standing.

## PART III — RETAILER

### Section 4 — Retailer Role Definition

**4.1** A "**Retailer**" is a Channel Partner operating at the last-mile customer interface, typically through a physical outlet, offering one or more of the following services: POS, AEPS, DMT, BBPS, Wallet Load, QR, and related payment services.

**4.2** Retailers may be onboarded directly by SAMEDAY or through a Distributor / Master Distributor upline.

### Section 5 — Retailer Obligations

**5.1 Operational Obligations:**

| S.No. | Obligation | Details |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 1 | Outlet Operations | Maintain a functional, accessible outlet during declared business hours |
| 2 | Customer Service | Assist customers with AEPS, DMT, BBPS, and QR transactions per SAMEDAY SOPs |
| 3 | KYC at Counter | Verify customer identity where required; refuse suspicious transactions |
| 4 | Receipt Issuance | Provide transaction receipts/acknowledgements to customers |
| 5 | Device Custody | Safeguard POS/mPOS/AEPS devices; report loss/theft immediately |
| 6 | Geo-Compliance | Operate devices only at registered geo-tagged location unless mobile device authorised |
| 7 | Cash Management | Maintain adequate cash float for AEPS/DMT; follow cash handling SOPs |
| 8 | Signage | Display SAMEDAY-approved branding and grievance helpline |
| 9 | Training | Ensure outlet staff complete compliance training |
| 10 | No Outsourcing | Proprietor/authorised person must supervise; no unauthorised third-party operation |

**5.2 Service-Specific Obligations:**

**(a) POS:** Accept card payments only on SAMEDAY-assigned terminals; do not process transactions for unrelated merchants; comply with PCI-DSS handling norms for card-present transactions.

**(b) AEPS:** Conduct transactions only with customer present and genuine biometric authentication; maintain AEPS handler certification; never process Fake AEPS or agent-assisted fraud.

**(c) DMT:** Verify remitter details; comply with KYC limits; refuse transfers to suspicious beneficiaries; maintain remittance records.

**(d) BBPS:** Collect bill payments only with valid customer authorisation; verify biller and consumer identifiers before processing.

**(e) Wallet Load:** Load wallets only from legitimate sources; verify source of funds for high-value loads.

**(f) QR:** Display assigned QR at registered outlet; do not share QR for remote/unauthorised collections.

**(g) Settlement / Instant Settlement:** Maintain accurate bank account; understand T+1 and instant settlement cut-offs per Annexure C.

**5.3 Prohibitions:** Retailers shall not act as Distributors, onboard downline partners, or negotiate commission structures with other partners unless expressly authorised.

**5.4 Commission:** Retailers earn commission as per Annexure C, subject to upline hierarchy where applicable.

## PART IV — DISTRIBUTOR

### Section 6 — Distributor Role Definition

**6.1** A "**Distributor**" is a Channel Partner authorised to recruit, manage, and support Retailers within an assigned territory, while optionally operating its own retail outlet(s).

**6.2** Distributors act as the first supervisory layer over Retailer conduct and compliance.

### Section 7 — Distributor Obligations

**7.1 Recruitment and Onboarding:**

**(a)** Onboard Retailers only through SAMEDAY's official onboarding process (DOC-1);

**(b)** Conduct preliminary KYC verification and outlet inspection before recommending activation;

**(c)** Not onboard Retailers with known fraud history, PEP concerns (without disclosure), or incomplete documentation;

**(d)** Maintain a register of all downline Retailers with contact details, outlet addresses, and activation dates.

**7.2 Supervision and Compliance:**

**(a)** Monitor downline transaction patterns for anomalies (velocity, split transactions, geographic irregularities);

**(b)** Conduct periodic outlet visits (minimum monthly for active Retailers);

**(c)** Ensure downline completes compliance training and displays required signage;

**(d)** Immediately report downline involvement in prohibited activities per Annexure A;

**(e)** Cooperate with SAMEDAY investigations involving downline partners.

**7.3 Downline Liability:**

**7.3.1** The Distributor shall be **jointly and severally liable** for:

**(a)** Penalties imposed on downline Retailers for Fake AEPS, split transactions, and critical violations where the Distributor failed to exercise reasonable supervision;

**(b)** Recovery of fraud losses attributable to downline where the Distributor negligently onboarded or failed to report known misconduct;

**(c)** KYC deficiencies in downline applications submitted through the Distributor's recommendation.

**7.3.2** Liability under Section 7.3.1 shall not exceed the actual loss/penalty amount plus investigation costs, and may be set off against Distributor commissions and settlements per Annexure A, Section 6.

**7.4 Operational Services:** Distributors may themselves operate POS, AEPS, DMT, BBPS, Wallet Load, QR, and Settlement services subject to the same obligations as Retailers under Section 5.

**7.5 Territory:** Distributors shall operate within assigned territory. Cross-territory recruitment requires SAMEDAY approval.

**7.6 Reporting:** Distributors shall submit monthly downline activity reports when requested, including new onboardings, terminations, and compliance incidents.

## PART V — MASTER DISTRIBUTOR

### Section 8 — Master Distributor Role Definition

**8.1** A "**Master Distributor**" ("**MD**") is a Channel Partner authorised to recruit and manage Distributors and Retailers (directly or through Distributors) within a defined region, with enhanced commission structures and supervisory responsibilities.

**8.2** MDs represent the highest tier in the retail hierarchy below SAMEDAY.

### Section 9 — Master Distributor Obligations

**9.1** MDs shall perform all Distributor obligations under Section 7, applied to their entire downline tree (Distributors and Retailers).

**9.2 Additional MD Obligations:**

| S.No. | Obligation | Details |
|-------|------------|---------|
| 1 | Distributor Management | Recruit, train, and monitor Distributors; ensure Distributors perform outlet visits |
| 2 | Regional Compliance | Appoint a compliance liaison for the region; respond to SAMEDAY within 24 hours |
| 3 | Volume Reporting | Provide aggregated volume and growth reports as requested |
| 4 | Escalation | First-level escalation point for fraud alerts, device issues, and settlement disputes |
| 5 | Policy Cascade | Ensure all circulars, policy updates, and rate changes are communicated to downline within 48 hours |
| 6 | Audit Cooperation | Facilitate regional audits and mystery shopping across downline network |
| 7 | Minimum Network | Maintain minimum active downline count as specified in onboarding approval letter |

**9.3 Downline Liability (MD):**

**9.3.1** MDs bear **enhanced downline liability** for the entire network under their MD code, including:

**(a)** Systemic fraud patterns across multiple downline outlets indicating failure of supervisory controls;

**(b)** Repeated critical violations (Fake AEPS, money laundering indicators) within the MD network;

**(c)** Failure to terminate or report rogue downline after written warning from SAMEDAY;

**(d)** Collusion or wilful blindness in downline misconduct.

**9.3.2** SAMEDAY may hold MD settlements to secure potential recovery amounts during investigation of downline fraud affecting multiple outlets.

**9.3.3** MD liability is cumulative with Distributor liability where both tiers failed in supervision; SAMEDAY may recover from either or both.

**9.4** MDs shall not create sub-MD tiers or unauthorised regional franchises without written agreement.

## PART VI — PARTNER (API)

### Section 10 — Partner (API) Role Definition

**10.1** A "**Partner (API)**" is a Channel Partner that integrates with SAMEDAY's platform via application programming interfaces (APIs) to offer payment and financial services programmatically to its own user base, subject to technical and compliance certification.

**10.2** API Partners operate under a distinct integration agreement supplement covering API keys, webhooks, SLAs, and security requirements.

### Section 11 — Partner (API) Obligations

**11.1 Technical Obligations:**

| S.No. | Requirement | Details |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Secure Integration | Implement TLS 1.2+, secure key storage, IP whitelisting |
| 2 | API Key Custody | Protect API keys; rotate on compromise; no sharing with third parties |
| 3 | Webhook Handling | Acknowledge callbacks within [Webhook SLA] seconds |
| 4 | Idempotency | Implement idempotent transaction requests to prevent duplicates |
| 5 | Error Handling | Graceful handling of timeouts, reversals, and error codes per API docs |
| 6 | UAT Sign-off | Complete UAT before production access |
| 7 | Change Management | Notify SAMEDAY before infrastructure/IP changes |
| 8 | Logging | Maintain API logs for minimum eight (8) years |

**11.2 Compliance Obligations:**

**(a)** Perform KYC on end users as required for each service (DMT, Wallet Load, etc.);

**(b)** Implement transaction monitoring equivalent to SAMEDAY thresholds;

**(c)** Not white-label services to unauthorised sub-aggregators without SAMEDAY approval;

**(d)** Report security breaches within six (6) hours of discovery;

**(e)** Submit to annual security audit or PCI/ISO assessment if handling card data.

**11.3 Service Entitlements:** API Partners may access subsets of: DMT, BBPS, Wallet Load, QR, Settlement, and Instant Settlement based on integration scope approved in writing. POS and AEPS are typically retail-outlet services and require separate retail onboarding unless API-based AEPS is expressly enabled.

**11.4 Liability:** API Partners are liable for all transactions initiated via their API credentials, including fraud attributable to their integration, weak KYC, or credential compromise due to negligence.

## PART VIII — POS DEVICE CUSTODY CHAIN

### Section 14 — Device Ownership and Allocation

**14.1** POS and mPOS devices supplied by SAMEDAY or its vendors remain the property of SAMEDAY or the designated lessor until title transfer is expressly agreed in writing.

**14.2** Device allocation follows the custody chain below:

| Stage | Custodian | Responsibility |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | SAMEDAY / Vendor | Inventory, configuration, software licensing |
| 2 | Master Distributor | Regional stock (if approved as device custodian) |
| 3 | Distributor | Territory stock; delivery to Retailers |
| 4 | Retailer | End-user custody; daily operation and physical security |
| 5 | Return | Retailer → Distributor → SAMEDAY upon termination/replacement |

**14.3** Each transfer in the custody chain shall be logged in SAMEDAY's device management system with serial number, date, and acknowledging party.

### Section 15 — Device Handling Obligations

**15.1** All custodians in the chain shall:

**(a)** Verify device serial number and seal integrity upon receipt;

**(b)** Not install unauthorised software or hardware modifications;

**(c)** Report malfunction within forty-eight (48) hours;

**(d)** Return device with all accessories (charger, cable, SIM if applicable) upon request;

**(e)** Bear penalty for loss/negligence as per Annexure A (₹16,000 + GST for POS device loss).

**15.2** Retailers shall not relocate devices to unregistered addresses without prior SAMEDAY approval and re-geo-tagging.

**15.3** Distributors and MDs distributing devices shall verify outlet existence before handover and obtain signed device acknowledgement from Retailers.
