## 3. CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA COLLECTED

We may collect the following categories of personal data depending on your role and services availed:

### 3.1 Agent / Partner Data

- Legal name, trade name, constitution, date of incorporation/establishment
- PAN, GSTIN, shop establishment registration
- Registered and operational addresses
- Bank account details (account number, IFSC, cancelled cheque)
- Authorized signatory name, designation, photograph, signature specimen
- Contact details: mobile, email, landline
- KYC documents: Aadhaar (masked where applicable), voter ID, passport, driving licence
- Business proofs: electricity bill, rent agreement, shop photos, GPS coordinates
- Financial information: turnover declarations, commission statements, wallet balance
- Device allocation records, IP addresses, login logs
- Downline register and sub-agent KYC (where applicable)

### 3.2 End Customer Data (processed via Agent/platform)

- Name, mobile number, email (if provided)
- KYC documents for transaction thresholds (Aadhaar reference, PAN, etc.)
- Aadhaar authentication XML/reference numbers (biometric data processed only on certified devices per UIDAI norms; **We do not store raw biometric templates**)
- Transaction details: amount, date, type, beneficiary details, bill details
- Device/terminal ID, IP address, geolocation (where enabled)
- Complaint and grievance correspondence
- CCTV or photographic evidence where submitted for dispute resolution

### 3.3 Technical & Usage Data

- API logs, session tokens (hashed), device identifiers
- Browser type, operating system, app version
- Audit trails, OTP logs, consent timestamps
- Cookies and similar technologies on web dashboards (as per Cookie Policy)

### 3.4 Sensitive Personal Data

We minimize collection of sensitive personal data. Where required by law or banking partners (e.g., AEPS authentication), processing occurs through certified channels. We do not store full card track data, CVV, or raw biometric templates on Our servers.

## 5. LEGAL BASIS AND CONSENT

5.1 Under the DPDP Act, 2023, We process personal data based on:

- **Consent** of the Data Principal (Agent, signatory, or End Customer as applicable);
- **Legitimate uses** permitted under the DPDP Act, including compliance with law, responding to medical/security emergencies, employment purposes (for employees), and certain public interest scenarios as prescribed;
- **Compliance with Applicable Law** including PMLA, RBI Master Directions, and contractual necessity under the MSA.

5.2 Agents must obtain valid, informed, specific, and unambiguous consent from End Customers before collecting and sharing their data with the Company.

5.3 Consent may be withdrawn subject to contractual and legal retention obligations; withdrawal may limit ability to provide Services.

## 7. DATA RETENTION — 8 YEARS

7.1 We retain personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Notice and to comply with legal obligations.

7.2 **Minimum Retention Period:** Transaction records, KYC documents, audit logs, and compliance-related personal data shall be retained for a minimum of **eight (8) years** from the date of transaction, relationship termination, or as prescribed under PMLA, RBI, and income tax laws, whichever is longer.

7.3 After retention period expiry, data shall be securely deleted, anonymized, or archived in accordance with Our data retention policy.

7.4 Data subject to litigation, investigation, or regulatory hold may be retained until resolution.

---

## 8. DATA SECURITY

8.1 We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures including encryption in transit (TLS/HTTPS), access controls, role-based permissions, logging, and periodic security assessments.

8.2 Agents shall implement equivalent safeguards at their premises, including secure storage of KYC copies, password protection, and restricted access to Devices and dashboards.

8.3 No system is completely secure; We shall not be liable for breaches caused by Agent negligence, credential sharing, or third-party attacks beyond Our reasonable control, subject to Applicable Law.

---

## 9. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION — 24 HOURS

9.1 Any known or suspected personal data breach affecting Company systems or data shared with the Company must be reported by the Agent to **privacy@samedaysolutions.in** within **twenty-four (24) hours** of discovery.

9.2 Report must include: nature of breach, categories of data affected, approximate number of individuals affected, likely consequences, and remedial measures taken.

9.3 We shall assess the breach and notify the Data Protection Board of India and affected Data Principals as required under the DPDP Act and applicable rules.

9.4 Failure by Agent to timely report breaches may result in suspension, penalties, and indemnity claims under the MSA.

| Right | Description |

| **Erasure** | Request deletion where retention is not legally required |

Certain requests may be declined where retention is mandated by PMLA, RBI, tax, or ongoing legal proceedings.
