## RECITALS

**A.** SAMEDAY SOLUTIONS PRIVATE LIMITED ("**SAMEDAY**" or the "**Company**") operates as a fintech channel partner platform offering payment and financial inclusion services including POS, AEPS, DMT, BBPS, Wallet Load, QR, Settlement, and Instant Settlement.

**B.** Channel Partners act as independent intermediaries and are responsible for ensuring that all transactions conducted through SAMEDAY's platform comply with applicable laws, RBI/NPCI guidelines, and this Policy.

**C.** This Annexure A forms an integral part of the Channel Partner Agreement and is binding upon execution of the onboarding form (DOC-1) and the principal agreement.

---

## SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

**1.1 Definitions.** In this Policy, unless the context otherwise requires:

**(a) "Channel Partner"** means any Retailer, Distributor, Master Distributor, or Partner (API) onboarded by SAMEDAY.

**(b) "Prohibited Transaction"** means any transaction, activity, or conduct listed in Section 2 or otherwise declared prohibited by SAMEDAY, NPCI, RBI, or applicable law.

**(c) "Fake AEPS"** means any Aadhaar Enabled Payment System transaction that is fabricated, unauthorised, conducted without genuine customer presence, uses forged biometrics, or is otherwise not a bona fide customer-initiated transaction.

**(d) "Split Transaction"** means structuring or dividing a single underlying transaction into multiple smaller transactions to evade limits, monitoring thresholds, or regulatory reporting requirements.

**(e) "Suspicious Transaction"** means any transaction that gives rise to a reasonable ground of suspicion that it may involve proceeds of crime, money laundering, terrorist financing, or violation of applicable law.

**(f) "Downline"** means any sub-partner, retailer, or agent onboarded directly or indirectly by a Distributor or Master Distributor.

**1.2 Interpretation.** Headings are for convenience only. References to statutory provisions include amendments and re-enactments. Words importing the singular include the plural and vice versa.

## SECTION 2 — PROHIBITED TRANSACTIONS AND ACTIVITIES

**2.1 General Prohibition.** No Channel Partner shall initiate, facilitate, assist, or permit any Prohibited Transaction through SAMEDAY's platform, systems, devices, or APIs.

**2.2 Prohibited Transactions Table.** The following table is illustrative and non-exhaustive. SAMEDAY may update this list by written notice:

| S.No. | Prohibited Activity / Transaction | Description | Severity |
|-------|-----------------------------------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | Fake AEPS Transactions | Fabricated or non-genuine AEPS cash withdrawal/deposit without customer consent and biometric authentication | Critical |
| 2 | Split Transactions | Breaking transactions to circumvent daily/monthly limits or monitoring | Critical |
| 3 | Money Laundering / Layering | Transactions designed to disguise origin of illicit funds | Critical |
| 4 | Terrorist Financing | Any transaction linked to proscribed entities or terror funding | Critical |
| 5 | Account Opening Fraud | Use of forged KYC, synthetic identities, or impersonation | Critical |
| 6 | Card Skimming / Cloning | Capture or misuse of card/PIN data via POS or other means | Critical |
| 7 | Unauthorised Cash-Out | Converting wallet/electronic value to cash without underlying legitimate transaction | High |
| 8 | Circular Transactions | Funds routed in circular manner among related accounts without economic purpose | High |
| 9 | Exceeding Regulatory Limits | Breach of PPI, DMT, AEPS, or other scheme limits | High |
| 10 | Third-Party Operation | Allowing unauthorised persons to operate partner ID, device, or outlet | High |
| 11 | Misuse of BBPS | Payment of bills without customer authorisation or for illegitimate purposes | High |
| 12 | DMT to Prohibited Categories | Transfers to gambling, crypto exchanges (where prohibited), or sanctioned entities | Critical |
| 13 | QR / UPI Misrouting | Static QR misuse, fake merchant QR, or transaction laundering via QR | High |
| 14 | Wallet Load Abuse | Loading wallets from suspicious sources or for onward illegal transfer | High |
| 15 | Device Tampering | Physical or software tampering of POS/AEPS/mPOS devices | Critical |
| 16 | Credential Sharing | Sharing login credentials, API keys, or terminal passwords | High |
| 17 | Commission Manipulation | Fraudulent commission claims, fake onboarding, or ghost merchants | High |
| 18 | Regulatory Evasion | Any act intended to evade STR/CTR reporting or KYC requirements | Critical |

**2.3 Zero Tolerance.** Critical-severity prohibited activities may result in immediate suspension, blacklisting, and legal action without prior notice, in addition to monetary penalties under Section 9.

## SECTION 3 — TRANSACTION MONITORING AND SURVEILLANCE

**3.1 Monitoring Systems.** SAMEDAY maintains automated and manual transaction monitoring systems to detect unusual patterns, velocity breaches, geographic anomalies, duplicate biometrics, and other red flags.

**3.2 Channel Partner Obligations.** Each Channel Partner shall:

**(a)** Monitor its own transactions and downline (where applicable) for suspicious activity;

**(b)** Cooperate with SAMEDAY's requests for transaction clarifications within twenty-four (24) hours or such shorter period as may be specified;

**(c)** Maintain records of customer consent, AEPS transaction receipts, and supporting documentation for a minimum period of eight (8) years or such longer period as prescribed by law;

**(d)** Not disable, circumvent, or interfere with SAMEDAY's monitoring tools, geo-fencing, or device management systems.

**3.3 Surveillance Measures.** SAMEDAY may, without prior notice:

**(a)** Conduct mystery shopping, outlet visits, and device inspections;

**(b)** Review CCTV footage where available and legally obtainable;

**((c)** Analyse IP logs, API call patterns, and device serial number mapping;

**(d)** Temporarily throttle or block transaction categories pending investigation.

**3.4 Escalation.** Detected anomalies shall be escalated to SAMEDAY's Compliance Officer and, where required, to the Principal Officer under PMLA and the sponsor bank's nodal officer.

---

## SECTION 4 — ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) AND KYC COMPLIANCE

**4.1 PMLA Compliance.** Channel Partners shall comply with the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, PMLA Rules, and RBI Master Direction on KYC, including customer identification, beneficial ownership disclosure, and ongoing due diligence.

**4.2 Re-KYC and Periodic Updates.** Channel Partners shall complete re-KYC and document refresh as and when requested by SAMEDAY or mandated by regulators, failing which services may be suspended.

**4.3 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs).** Enhanced due diligence shall apply to PEPs and their related parties. Channel Partners must disclose PEP status at onboarding and upon any subsequent change.

**4.4 Customer Due Diligence at Outlet Level.** Retailers and agents conducting AEPS, DMT, or wallet services shall verify customer identity where required, maintain transaction logs, and refuse service where identification is incomplete or suspicious.

**4.5 No Anonymity.** Channel Partners shall not facilitate anonymous or pseudonymous transactions beyond what is permitted under applicable RBI/NPCI frameworks.

---

## SECTION 5 — INVESTIGATION AND ENQUIRY PROCEDURE

**5.1 Initiation.** SAMEDAY may initiate investigation upon: (a) internal alert; (b) bank/NPCI/regulator referral; (c) customer complaint; (d) law enforcement request; or (e) whistleblower report.

**5.2 Interim Measures.** During investigation, SAMEDAY may:

**(a)** Suspend partner account, API access, or device;

**(b)** Hold settlement amounts to the extent of disputed or suspicious transactions;

**(c)** Require written explanation, supporting documents, and affidavit from the Channel Partner;

**(d)** Conduct personal hearing (in person or video) with the Channel Partner and/or its downline.

**5.3 Timeline.** Channel Partners shall respond to show-cause notices within three (3) business days unless extended in writing. Failure to respond may result in ex-parte determination.

**5.4 Outcome.** Upon conclusion, SAMEDAY may: (a) restore services; (b) impose penalties; (c) terminate the partnership; (d) report to authorities; or (e) initiate recovery proceedings under Section 6.

**5.5 Appeal.** A Channel Partner may submit a written appeal to [Compliance Email] within seven (7) calendar days of penalty/termination notice. Appeal does not automatically stay recovery or regulatory reporting obligations.

---

## SECTION 6 — RECOVERY AND SET-OFF

**6.1 Recovery Rights.** SAMEDAY shall be entitled to recover from the Channel Partner any amounts arising from:

**(a)** Penalties imposed under this Policy;

**(b)** Chargebacks, reversals, and fraud losses attributable to the Channel Partner or its downline;

**(c)** Regulatory fines or penalties imposed on SAMEDAY due to the Channel Partner's conduct;

**(d)** Costs of investigation, device replacement, and legal proceedings.

**6.2 Set-Off.** SAMEDAY may set off recovery amounts against: (a) pending settlements; (b) security deposits; (c) commission accruals; or (d) any other amounts due to the Channel Partner, without prejudice to additional legal remedies.

**6.3 Downline Liability.** Distributors and Master Distributors shall be jointly and severally liable for losses and penalties arising from their downline's prohibited activities unless conclusively demonstrated that such downline acted entirely outside the scope of authorised platform access.

**6.4 Limitation.** Recovery actions may be pursued for a period of eight (8) years from the date of discovery of the violation or such longer period as permitted by law.

---

## SECTION 7 — REGULATORY REPORTING

**7.1 Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR).** SAMEDAY shall file STRs with the Financial Intelligence Unit – India (FIU-IND) where required under PMLA, without tipping off the Channel Partner.

**7.2 Cash Transaction Reports (CTR).** Aggregated cash transactions exceeding prescribed thresholds shall be reported as per applicable norms.

**7.3 NPCI / Bank Reporting.** SAMEDAY shall report fraud, AEPS disputes, and scheme violations to NPCI, sponsor banks, and payment system providers as mandated.

**7.4 Law Enforcement Cooperation.** Channel Partners shall promptly provide all assistance, documents, and testimony requested by SAMEDAY in connection with regulatory or law enforcement enquiries.

**7.5 No Obstruction.** Channel Partners shall not destroy, alter, or conceal records during or after an investigation.

---

## SECTION 8 — BLACKLISTING AND TERMINATION

**8.1 Blacklisting.** SAMEDAY maintains an internal blacklist of Channel Partners, proprietors, directors, and beneficial owners involved in serious or repeated violations. Blacklisted persons shall not be re-onboarded without Compliance Committee approval.

**8.2 Industry Sharing.** SAMEDAY may share blacklist information with banks, industry associations, and other fintech platforms where legally permitted and under appropriate confidentiality agreements.

**8.3 Termination.** In addition to blacklisting, SAMEDAY may terminate the Channel Partner Agreement immediately for material breach of this Policy.

**8.4 Consequences of Termination.** Upon termination for cause:

**(a)** All devices must be returned within seven (7) calendar days;

**(b)** API keys and credentials shall be revoked immediately;

**(c)** Pending settlements may be held for up to ninety (90) days for fraud reconciliation;

**(d)** Downline may be reassigned or terminated at SAMEDAY's discretion.
